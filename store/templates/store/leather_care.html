The Kangarli Handbook

Leather Care Guide

Premium leather is a living material. With the right care, it deepens in character, develops a rich patina, and lasts generations. Here's how to honour your investment.

Exotic Tier · Crocodile

Crocodile Leather

The pinnacle of exotic leather. Crocodile skin features distinctive scale patterns that become more defined and lustrous with age. Each piece is entirely unique — no two hides share the same pattern.

Conditioning

Apply a specialist exotic leather conditioner every 3–4 months. Never use regular leather cream — it can soften the scales unevenly and cause irreversible distortion.

Sun & Heat

Keep away from direct sunlight. UV rays cause natural oils to evaporate rapidly, leading to cracking between the scales. Never leave near a radiator or in a hot car.

Cleaning

Use a barely damp soft cloth to remove dust and surface grime. Always wipe in the direction of the scales — never against them or you risk lifting the edges permanently.

Storage

Store in a breathable cloth dust bag, away from plastic. Stuff jackets lightly with acid-free tissue to maintain shape. Never hang from a single point — it stresses the shoulder seams.

Western Heritage · Bison

Bison Leather

Bison hide is thicker and more textured than cowhide, with a natural grain that tells the story of the animal's life. Exceptionally durable, it develops a beautiful rugged patina unlike any other leather.

Conditioning

Use a high-quality beeswax or neatsfoot oil conditioner every 2–3 months. Bison leather absorbs conditioner deeply due to its open grain — apply generously and buff off the excess.

Water Resistance

Apply a wax-based protector at the start of each season. If it gets thoroughly wet, allow it to air dry naturally at room temperature — never use heat.

Brushing

Use a firm horsehair brush to remove dust lodged in the deep grain. This also helps distribute natural body oils evenly across the entire surface over time.

Patina

Embrace the aging process entirely. Scratches on bison leather add character rather than damage — buff lightly with a soft cloth to blend them into the surrounding grain.

Velvet Touch · Suede

Suede Leather

Made from the inner split of the hide, suede has a soft, napped finish that feels uniquely luxurious. It requires more attentive maintenance than smooth leathers but rewards with unmatched texture and depth.

Water Protection

Suede stains easily with water. Apply a quality suede protector spray before first use and reapply every 4–6 weeks. This is non-negotiable for maintaining appearance.

Brushing

Use a dedicated suede brush to restore the nap after each wear. Always brush in one consistent direction to maintain the velvet-like texture and prevent matting.

Stain Removal

For dry stains, use a suede eraser with firm, short strokes. For oil stains, apply talcum powder and leave overnight to absorb the oil, then brush away completely.

Drying

If suede becomes wet, stuff the item with newspaper and allow to dry at room temperature only. Once fully dry, brush vigorously in one direction to fully restore the nap.

Classic Elegance · Calfskin

Calfskin Leather

The most refined of standard leathers. Calfskin is prized for its fine, tight grain and silky smooth surface. It ages with extraordinary grace, developing a subtle, deep shine with consistent care.

Polishing

Use a dedicated leather cream monthly. Apply in small circular motions with a soft lint-free cloth, allow to absorb for five minutes, then buff to a gentle shine with a horsehair brush.

Conditioning

Condition every 2 months to prevent the fine grain from drying and developing micro-cracks. Apply sparingly — a little goes a long way with calfskin's dense surface.

Daily Care

Wipe down after each use with a dry microfibre cloth. This removes accumulated body oils and perspiration that slowly degrade the surface finish over months of wear.

Colour Maintenance

Use colour-matched leather cream to maintain vibrancy and prevent fading. Avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight — UV light bleaches the surface colour gradually.

Rare & Bold · Python

Snake Leather

Python and other exotic snake skins are prized for their striking geometric scale patterns. Extremely thin yet surprisingly durable when treated correctly, snake leather demands specialist handling to preserve its singular beauty.

Scale Direction

Always stroke, clean and condition in the direction the scales naturally lie — from head to tail. Going against the grain will lift and loosen the edges, causing permanent irreversible damage.

Moisturising

Apply a specialist exotic leather oil very sparingly every 3–4 months. Snake skin dries out quickly, particularly around scale edges — but over-oiling causes the scales to separate.

Cleaning

Use only a soft, barely damp cloth to clean the surface. Never submerge or saturate snake leather — the thin membrane beneath the scales can warp and discolour irreversibly when wet.

Storage

Store flat or loosely rolled in a breathable cloth. Avoid sharp folds or creases — they crack the scales along the fold line permanently. Keep away from direct contact with other leathers.

Refined Texture · Nubuck

Nubuck Leather

Made from the outer grain side of the hide rather than the inner split, nubuck is more durable than suede while retaining a refined velvety appearance. The choice of discerning craftsmen worldwide for its superior wear resistance.

Protection First

Before first use, apply a nubuck-specific protector spray from 20–30cm distance. Allow to fully dry before wearing. This step is critical and should be repeated every 4–6 weeks.

Regular Brushing

Brush weekly with a dedicated nubuck brush to keep the nap raised and remove embedded dust. This prevents the surface texture from gradually flattening under pressure and wear.

Winter Care

Apply extra protection against road salt and rain in cold months. Salt deposits left untreated will permanently stain and harden nubuck — wipe away immediately with a damp cloth if contact occurs.

Restoring the Nap

If texture flattens with heavy use, hold steam from a kettle at 20cm distance for several seconds, then brush immediately and firmly in one direction to fully raise the fibres back.

Legendary Durability · Horsehide

Horsehide Leather

The choice of aviators and motorcyclists for generations. Horsehide is denser and more tightly grained than cowhide, exceptionally resistant to abrasion. It breaks in slowly but moulds perfectly to the wearer's body over time.

Break-In

New horsehide feels noticeably stiff — this is entirely normal and expected. Wear it consistently and it will mould perfectly to your body within 2–3 months of regular use.

Conditioning

Use a heavy-duty conditioner such as Leather Honey or Pecard's Antique Leather Dressing every 3 months. Horsehide is extremely dense and requires firm, generous application to penetrate properly.

Water Handling

Horsehide is naturally more water-resistant than cowhide due to its density. If thoroughly soaked, dry slowly at room temperature and condition thoroughly once completely dry.

Patina

Horsehide develops one of the most spectacular patinas of any leather on earth. The longer and harder you wear it, the more magnificent it becomes — never over-clean or over-condition.

Universal Leather Rules

Regardless of the hide, these principles apply to every piece in your collection. Master these fundamentals and your leather will outlast everything else you own.

01
Never Machine Wash

Water, heat and agitation destroy leather's natural fibre structure irreversibly. Always clean by hand using specialist products appropriate for the specific leather type.

02
Avoid Extreme Heat

Radiators, car dashboards in summer, and hairdryers cause leather to dry, crack and shrink. Air dry only, at room temperature, away from any heat source.

03
Store with Intention

Always use breathable cloth dust bags — never plastic, which traps moisture. Stuff jackets and bags to maintain shape. Keep in cool, dark, dry conditions year-round.

04
Condition Regularly

Leather dries out as natural oils evaporate over time. Conditioning every 2–3 months replenishes lost oils and prevents the premature cracking that shortens a piece's life dramatically.

05
Always Test First

Test any new product on a hidden interior panel before full application. Some conditioners and protectors darken or alter the surface colour of certain leathers permanently.

06
Honour the Patina

Minor scratches and wear marks are not damage — they are evidence of a life well lived. Premium leather is designed to age with the wearer. Never try to restore it to looking new.
